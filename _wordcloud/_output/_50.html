With many people blogging about what app’s they use as their default, and being inspired by Kev Quirk’s post on it, I thought I would list mine. I will try to explain a bit about why I’ve chosen each app, that way if you are looking for a new app one of these may work for you.

Mail Client: Thunderbird – can read email from any server all in one place, especially since I have multiple email address.

Mail Server: Runbox – email provider based in Norway that cares about privacy and the environment.

Mail Alias: Addy.io – what if you want to get an email from a website but don’t want to worry about giving your real email address or not being able to unsubscribe, then use an alias.

Notes: Notepad++ / Zenkit To Do – depending on where I am, I may use one of these to make a note.

To-Do / shopping list: Zenkit To Do – a very generous free plan.

Cloud Backup: Backblaze (affiliate link) – it’s always good to have a backup offsite that does last minute backups. I know it’s paid for itself when my computer hard drive has died before.

RSS: Inoreader – want to see new posts from a website without always having to check it, use an RSS reader.

Browser: Librewolf – Firefox but private, seriously no tracking and many customizations made to make it more private.

Read It Later: Wallabag – I have a huge read it later list that I really should start reading.

Book reading: Kobo eReader – I wanted an eReader that wasn’t Amazon and I could get a replacement for if needed

Word Processing / Spreadsheets / Presentations: Libreoffice – why pay Microsoft for something that there’s a great free version of? Yes, something may be harder to do, but free is hard to beat.

Music: Tidal – pay’s one of the highest to artists, I do find bug’s every once in a while, so it isn’t the best.

Password Management: 1Password – if you aren’t using a password manager, then you should.

Social Media: Mastodon & LinkedIn – while I check Mastodon almost every day, LinkedIn is one I’m checking a couple time per week.

Search: SearXNG – why rely on one search engine for the results when you can poll from many? And you don’t need to rely on one company to display the results.

Code Editor: VSCodium – the same as VS Code but completely open source and no tracking.

Development environment: Laragon – I’ve recently found this and while the app doesn’t get often update, you can update PHP, Node.js or any one of those yourself.

Weather: Weather Underground, AccuWeather, Environmental Canada – I check many sources for the weather and these three are the ones I check the most.

Package tracking: Parcels – no matter the shipping company, I can put in the tracking number and Parcels has been able to find it, along with Apple and Android apps.
